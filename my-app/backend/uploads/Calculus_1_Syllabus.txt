CALCULUS 1 - MATH 101
Course Syllabus - Fall 2026

Instructor: Dr. Sarah Mitchell
Office Hours: MWF 2:00-3:30 PM

Course Description:
This course introduces students to differential and integral calculus. Topics include limits, continuity, derivatives, applications of derivatives, antiderivatives, and definite integrals.

Learning Objectives:
- Understand the concept of limits and continuity
- Master differentiation techniques and rules
- Apply derivatives to solve optimization problems
- Understand the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus
- Compute definite and indefinite integrals

Required Materials:
- Calculus: Early Transcendentals (8th Edition) by James Stewart
- Scientific calculator (graphing calculator recommended)

Grading:
- Homework: 20%
- Quizzes: 20%
- Midterm Exam: 25%
- Final Exam: 35%

Weekly Topics:
Week 1-2: Limits and Continuity
Week 3-4: Derivatives and Differentiation Rules
Week 5-6: Applications of Derivatives
Week 7-8: Integration Basics
Week 9-10: Applications of Integration
